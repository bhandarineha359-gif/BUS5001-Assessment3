# NotebookLM Evaluation Notes

## Project
BUS5001 Assessment 3 – Q4 Evaluating Cloud-Based Technology

## Tool Evaluated
Google NotebookLM

## Evaluation Scenario
The evaluation was conducted using an ESG Sustainability Reporting scenario. Multiple sustainability-related documents were uploaded into NotebookLM and tested to determine its usefulness in academic and professional environments.

---

## Scenario 1 – ESG Policy Summary

### Objective
Summarise a lengthy sustainability policy document.

### Observations
- NotebookLM produced a concise summary quickly.
- Key sustainability objectives were correctly identified.
- Important policy sections were referenced back to source documents.
- Responses were easy to understand.

### Strengths
- Fast document summarisation.
- Source-grounded responses.
- Reduced reading time.

### Limitations
- Some detailed policy requirements were omitted.
- Users still need to verify important compliance information.

---

## Scenario 2 – Research Preparation

### Objective
Use NotebookLM to prepare notes for an ESG assignment.

### Observations
- Generated organised study notes.
- Identified major ESG themes and concepts.
- Created useful revision material.

### Strengths
- Improved learning efficiency.
- Helpful for exam preparation.
- Simplified complex content.

### Limitations
- Risk of over-reliance on AI-generated notes.
- Students may skip reading original materials.

---

## Scenario 3 – Question and Answer Support

### Objective
Ask questions about uploaded sustainability reports.

### Observations
- Responses were generated quickly.
- Citations linked answers to uploaded sources.
- Questions could be refined to obtain more detailed answers.

### Strengths
- Interactive document exploration.
- Easy retrieval of information.
- Supports evidence-based learning.

### Limitations
- Quality depends on uploaded documents.
- Ambiguous questions sometimes produced incomplete answers.

---

## Scenario 4 – Comparative Analysis

### Objective
Compare information from multiple ESG-related documents.

### Observations
- NotebookLM identified similarities and differences effectively.
- Generated structured comparisons.
- Supported analytical thinking.

### Strengths
- Useful for report writing.
- Helps identify trends and themes.
- Saves significant research time.

### Limitations
- Comparisons occasionally lacked deeper critical analysis.
- Human judgement remains necessary.

---

## Overall Evaluation

### Accuracy
NotebookLM generally produced accurate outputs when answers were based on uploaded sources. Source citations improved transparency and trustworthiness.

### Usefulness
The platform is highly useful for:
- Assignment preparation
- Research summaries
- Literature reviews
- Policy analysis
- ESG reporting activities

### Limitations and Risks
- Potential over-reliance on AI-generated content.
- Users must verify important findings.
- Biases present in source documents may influence outputs.
- Critical thinking remains essential.

### Recommendation
NotebookLM is suitable for limited deployment within university learning environments. It provides significant productivity benefits for students and researchers, particularly when analysing large volumes of documents. However, outputs should always be reviewed by users and treated as decision-support rather than authoritative conclusions.

## Conclusion
NotebookLM demonstrated strong capabilities in document summarisation, question answering, note generation, and comparative analysis. The tool can enhance academic productivity and support ESG-related research activities while requiring appropriate human oversight.
